Strong technical work can still fail to land if the communication around it is vague, overloaded, or too abstract for the audience using it.

A better pattern is to explain:

1. what problem the work addresses
2. what tradeoffs were made
3. what someone should do differently because of the result

That structure keeps the writing grounded in decisions rather than drifting into detail for its own sake.
